Undergraduate Programs

International Business

The Department of Finance and International Business offers courses in two majors (Finance, and International Business). The International Business program provides a broad global business education with emphasis on language proficiency and study abroad that prepares the student for a wide variety of international business careers in domestic and international firms, government agencies, and international organizations. The program encourages study abroad programs and enhance international student exchange programs. Ralph J. Bunche International Affairs Center handles Study Abroad Program and International Affairs Study fellowship and scholarship. IB Program at Howard is one of the oldest in the US with its location in the nation’s capital, which is home to many international organizations, NGOs, and embassies. This proximity to a rich international environment provides students with ample experience of international exposure and international internship opportunities.

The International Business Program has two concentrations: Functional and Regional. Functional one includes Finance, Risk Management & Insurance, Marketing, Accounting, Management, Information Systems, Supply Chain Management, and so on; and Regional one covers Emerging Nations, Europe, Latin America, Middle East/North Africa, and Sub-Saharan Africa.
