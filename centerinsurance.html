Center for Insurance Education

The Center for Insurance Education (CIE) was created in 1978 to accommodate the insurance industryâ€™s need for better-educated employees in the field. The CIE is the portal through which the insurance industry accesses the more than 1500 students in the School of Business each year. The Howard University Center for Insurance Education interacts with and secures financial support from many fine institutions and organizations that are dedicated to the fulfillment of the CIE purpose. Support has been provided by: Aetna, AIG, Allstate, American Family, Aon, Chubb, CNA, HAI Group, Hartford, Marsh, Nationwide, Northwestern Mutual, Principal Financial, Prudential, State Farm, Towers Watson, Travelers, The American College, The Institutes, Society of Actuaries/Casualty Actuarial Society, International Association of Black Actuaries (IABA), Casualty Actuaries of the Mid-Atlantic Region (CAMAR), National African America Insurance Association (NAAIA), RIMS, CPCU Chapters, CLU/ChFC chapters, MDRT and many others.

The activities of the Center are designed to accomplish the goal of exposing highly qualified students with the opportunities available in the Insurance & Financial Services industries include:

  • Recruiting new students from external and internal sources
  • Recognizing the achievement of students
  • Awarding scholarships to deserving students
  • Developing corporate partnerships to achieve our goals
  • Raising funds for Center activities.
