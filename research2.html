Research & Insights

Quick Facts

Howard University School of Business was the first business school in Washington, DC to be accredited by the Association of Advance Collegiate Schools of Business (AACSB), the international accrediting body for both undergraduate and graduate business programs.
Dr. Barron Harvey has been the Dean of Howard University School of Business since 1994.
The Howard University School of Business retention rate is approximately 90%.
The average total compensation package for Howard School of Business graduating MBAs exceeds $105,000.
The average salary for students graduating from the undergraduate program exceeds $52,000 with over a $6,000 signing bonus.
Howard University School of Business has a five year graduation rate of nearly 60% and a four-year graduation rate of 53%.
The average SAT scores for Howard University School of Business incoming students is 1085.
School of Business faculty has a student faculty ratio of approximately 16:1. The faculty is comprised of 65 full-time faculty, and 9 adjunct instructors.
There ten and eight academic concentrations at Howard University School of Business undergraduate and graduate level respectively.
The School of Business offers Insurance as an undergraduate concentration.
Howard University School of Business has more African-American alumni that are employed by Wall Street firms, have become Certified Public Accountants, and are represented at top graduate schools than any other university in the country.
Howard University School of Business has a library that is rated among the best in the Washington metropolitan area.
Howard University School of Business has a 300-seat auditorium that is the preferred campus site for speaking engagements and is host to the schools Executive Lecture Series.
