Dr. Choudhury currently is the Professor Emeritus, in the Department of Marketing effective July 1, 2014. He joined the Department of Marketing as Assistant Professor of Marketing in the fall of 1974. Over the years he rose through the ranks and became full professor and first holder of John E. Jacob Chair Endowed Chair professor of Marketing. He was also the first Chairman of the Department of Marketing when the department was created in the fall of 1979.

He received his MBA from Atlanta University, MS and Ph.D. from University of California, Los Angeles. He has over the years, published over 70 papers in various journals including, the Journal of Advertising Research, Journal of the Academy of Marketing Science, Journal of International Consumer Marketing, Services Marketing Quarterly, Latin American Business Review, Journal of Macro-Marketing, Journal of Innovating Marketing, Journal of Marketing Theory and Practice, Journal of Bank Research, Multinational Business Review, Journal of Research on Minority Affairs and various national and regional conference proceedings.

Dr. Choudhury recently co-authored a book “Enhancing the Managerial DNA of Your Small Business,” published by Business Expert Press (May 2014). He is also the co-author of (1) Monographs on Pricing, and (2) Marketing to Ethnic Consumers: An Annotated Bibliography: (August 2000), featured as book of the month in April 2001 by the American Marketing Association. The American Marketing Association published these books.

Currently he is the Founding and Chief Editor of the “Journal of International Marketing Strategy,” published by Modern Technology and Management Institute Inc., USA, spring 2013. ISBN 2152-5307. The second issue of the Journal has already been published in Fall 2014. Dr. Choudhury has served as a Keynote Speaker, National Program Chairman, Proceedings Editor, and Co-Proceedings Editor, Track Chair and Session Chair of various national and regional conferences. He also serves as an Advisor of the Editorial Review Board, Journal of Effective Management, and International Islamic University Malaysia, and Journal of Case Studies in Management.

Dr. Choudhury’ s current research interests are in the area of Minority Consumers, Cross Cultural Analysis, Role of Marketing in Business & Economic Development, Global Marketing and Export Strategy, Marketing Strategies for Small Businesses including Franchising and Marketing Strategy Formulation and Implementation. Dr. Choudhury frequently conducts seminars in major U.S. cities, on Marketing Strategies for Small and 8(a) certified businesses, Franchisees/ Franchisers and on Internet Marketing Techniques.

Dr. Choudhury also served for 18 years, as a Program Manager, Small Business, HBCU (Historically Black Colleges and Universities), Minority Institutions (MIs), and Mentor Protégé Programs, with Department of Defense, Pentagon (MDA-Missile Defense Agency), where he held “Top Secret Security” clearance. In this capacity, he dealt with developing and reviewing public policy, congressional issues, and managed funds ($4 million a year) for distribution to HBCUs/MIs.

He also serves as consultant to various universities, conducted seminars, taught courses, and served as visiting scholar/researcher/advisor/external examiner of doctoral and master’s theses in India, Malaysia and Philippines, Fiji (University of South Pacific), and Mona School of Business, Kingston, Jamaica. He has also widely traveled to places like Vietnam, China, Brazil, Philippines, Malaysia and Singapore on educational tours and lectures.
