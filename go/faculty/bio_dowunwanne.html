Biography

Dr. Daniel N. Owunwanne joined the Department of Information Systems & Decision Sciences in 2003, after serving as Adjunct Professor at the University of the District of Columbia, Washington DC, and Instructor at Elizabeth City State University, NC.

Research Interest: His primary research areas of interest include: Data Interoperability in Federated Distributed Database Systems, Software Implemented Fault Tolerance, Error Detection in Data Transmission, and Cloud Computing.

Awards: His awards include: Software Engineering Certificate from Georgia State University; Computer Science Fellowship from NASA, Langley Research Center in Hampton, Virginia; 2006-2007 Professor of the Year Certificate; Certificate of Special Congressional Recognition; and The U.S. President’s 2007 Volunteer Service Award.

Professional Membership: Dr. Owunwanne is a member of a number of professional organizations which include: Interface of North America and Classification Society of North America, International Digital Business Academy, Virginia Academy of Science, Association of Computing Machinery, Special Interest Group on Computer Graphics, and National Association of Mathematics.

Courses Taught: He has taught: Database Management, Systems Analysis and Design, Information Structures, Management Information Systems, Computer Introduction, Software Design, Data Communications, C++, and Java Programming Language.

Education: Dr. Owunwanne holds a Ph.D. from George Mason University, VA; M.S. from Howard University, Washington DC; and B.S. from State University College, Buffalo, NY.
