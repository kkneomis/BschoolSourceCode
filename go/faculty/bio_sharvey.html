Biography

Almost immediately after graduating from Fairfield University in 1972, he began work in the University Office of Student Affairs as a counselor to minority students. In 1976, he left Fairfield to become Director of Minority Student Affairs at Georgetown University. During more than twenty years at Georgetown, he held positions as Assistant to the President for Urban Affairs and Federal Relations (1985-88) and Vice President for Urban Affairs (1988-96). While at Georgetown, he served on numerous university and community boards including being a founding member, in 1981, of the Conference on Minority Affairs of the Association of Jesuit Colleges and Universities.
