Biography

Dr. Lacy has been affiliated with the Howard University School of Business for over 30 years as a faculty member, administrator, researcher, advisor and trainer. She has also held several administrative positions at Howard: Associate Provost, Senior Academic Administrator in the Office of the Vice President for Academic Affairs; and Vice President for Administration. Dr. Lacy has also served as Assistant Dean for the School of Business. She is presently the Chair of the Department of Management, Labor Relations and Hospitality, School of Business, serving a seventh term (21 years). She also served as the Director of the Institute for Labor-Management Relations at the School of Business, where, among other duties, she directed a graduate MBA program designed to train professionals in the field of labor relations.

Dr. Lacy teaches courses in the subject areas of human resources management, general management, leadership and supervision, labor-management relations, organizational behavior, organizational development and change, and heads a special program in diversity and women in business. She has received numerous teaching awards, including several “Teacher of the Year” awards from the School of Business.

Dr. Lacy is a lead trainer for the Howard University Professional Development and Leadership Academy, the university’s training arm for employees. She is nationally known for her workshops, seminars, and consulting in management and leadership, human resources management, labor relations, conflict resolution, organizational change and valuing diversity. Her client list includes the US Departments of Defense, Justice, Agriculture, Commerce and Veterans’ Affairs; the Social Security Administration, the National Labor Relations Board, Small Business Administration and GAO; the cities of Washington, D.C. and Baltimore, Md., as well as private sector organizations such as AT&T, Fannie Mae, Ford Motor Company, the International Institute for Education, the National Alliance of Postal and Federal Employees, the Hospital for Sick Children, and Howard University Hospital, to name a few.

In addition to her work at Howard, Dr. Lacy serves as a Senior Fellow for the Center for Excellence in Municipal Management at George Washington University, where she conducts research and assists in the design and delivery of training programs for middle managers, principals, administrators and teachers of the District of Columbia and mid-level managers for the Council of Governments in the DC Metro area. She is also involved in a program at Georgetown University that assists former professional athletes with leadership and career development.

A native Washingtonian, Dr. Lacy is a product of the DC Public Schools. She received her BA degree from Lincoln University (Pa.), magna cum laude, in Economics and Business Administration (Accounting). She holds a MBA degree in Management and a Ph.D. degree in Industrial Relations from the University of Wisconsin (Madison).
