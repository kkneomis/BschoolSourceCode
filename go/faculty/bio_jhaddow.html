James Haddow is the Director of the Center for Excellence in Supply Chain Management (CESUM) and a professor in the School of Business at Howard University in Washington DC. Currently, Mr. Haddow teaches a range of supply chain management courses to both undergraduate and graduate level students. He also supports student teams as faculty advisor during supply chain case competitions and participates in various CESCM activities (e.g., Thursday Think Tanks, Corporate Advisory Board, Site Visits, etc.).

Mr. Haddow has previously held positions as Director of Global Procurement and a Supply Chain consultant for A.T. Kearney a global consulting firm, Cost and Financial Analyst at the Interstate Commerce Commission and Safeway Stores, Inc. His 30 years of consulting and industry experience encompass global procurement, supply chain management, business planning and commercial business development which provides real world teaching examples for his students.
