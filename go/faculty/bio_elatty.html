Biography

Professor Latty is passionate about emerging markets and emerging domestic markets private equity, securitizations, and minority participation in these spaces. He is co-founder and managing director of Corpelligence, a private equity firm that focuses on middle market investments in Latin America and the Caribbean (LAC). Professor Latty has worked on large private equity telecommunications and media transactions in the U.S. and in LAC. He is the former chairman of iDeal Decisions, Inc., an information technology firm; a board member of Recombinix, LLC, a biotechnology firm; and director at FMDI, a transport and logistics firm. Professor Latty has also worked on CMBS and CDO securitizations products at Criimi Mae, a former leader in the commercial mortgage-backed securities subordinate bond acquisition market. He was a summer intern at Merrill Lynch. He previously was general manager of an investment bank for an insurance group in the Caribbean where he conducted private placements, acquisitions, valuations, and other advisory roles. He also assisted a Caribbean government in their takeover of a distressed commercial bank.

Professor Latty obtained his MBA in Finance from the University of Pittsburgh, an MS in Biotechnology from the Johns Hopkins University, a previous student at Harvard University, and completed options trading courses at Carnegie Mellon University. He is a Thomas Jefferson Fellow and serves as a member on the Silver Spring Transportation Management District Advisory Committee. Professor Latty teaches Venture Capital & Private Equity, a graduate course in the School of Business at Howard University.
