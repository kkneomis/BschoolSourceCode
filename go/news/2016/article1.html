A DREAM COME TRUE: HOWARD STUDENT TO INTERN FOR NBC AT 2016 OLYMPIC GAMES

  • January 24, 2016

A Howard University student is more than elated about his distinctive internship plans for the 2016 summer. Jordan Culpepper, a sophomore international business major, will be heading to Brazil July 27 to participate in an intense, rewarding internship with NBC at the Olympic Games.

“If the position abroad is a career opportunity, it will give a recent grad a leg up having international exposure,” said Brenda Eribo, senior director of human resources at Council for Advancement and Support of Education (CASE). “The experience and skills learned will be different from studying abroad or learning another language and will give the student an advantage when he gets in his profession.”

A three-week internship based in Rio de Janiero, the NBC Olympics Guide Internship program gives students the opportunity to serve as the “face” of NBC. Interacting daily with NBC and Comcast executives and top advertising clients, interns will assist with airport meet and greets, event set-up, directional support, coordination of activity schedule and sign-ups, guest services, transportation assistance and much more.

“With those job duties, we [interns] are gaining critical thinking skills that will allow us to be the first line of defense when something goes wrong,” Culpepper said. “I’ve done international traveling before, but I know the level of alertness [in Brazil] will be quite different.”

The internship program was far from effortless, providing each intern with pay, travel and housing accommodations. In addition to submitting a resume, cover letter and detailed application, Culpepper had three interviews with NBC representatives over a span of nine months.

Only approximately 44 students were selected for the NBC Campus 2 Career Internship Program out of a pool of over 2,000 applicants. With low employment rates and competitiveness at an all-time-high, Tonija Navas, deputy director of the Ralph Bunche International Affairs Center at Howard University, is confident the internship engagement abroad will bode well for his future.

This story originally appeared on The Hilltop Newspaper on January 24, 2016. Written by Will Fuller.
